    • If h \ne 0, the system has a unique solution with z = \frac{i}{h}.
    • If h = 0 and i \ne 0, the last row reads 0 \times z = i. Hence there's no solution and the system is inconsistent.
    • If h = 0 and i = 0, the last row has all zeros. Hence there’re infinitely many solutions of the form x = p+kt, y = q+lt, and z= t.

Possible Arrangements of Planes

Two planes

  1. Intersecting at a line
  2. Parallel
  3. Coincident (P_1 = P_2)

Three Planes

  1. All coincident (P_1 = P_2 = P_3)
  2. Two coincident and one intersecting at a line
  3. Two coincident and one parallel
  4. Two parallel and one intersecting
  5. All three parallel
  6. All meet at one point
  7. All meet in a common line
  8. The line of intersection of any two is parallel to the third plane
